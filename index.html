The 3 Core Business Models

Lender Platform

Offers one financing product from a single source (often a bank). Simple and consistent, but with lower approval rates.

Pros: Simple, consistent branding.

Cons: Lower approval rates, less flexibility.

Key Players:

Goodleap Synchrony Service Finance

Aggregator

One platform connects to a curated network of lenders, creating a "waterfall" to boost approval chances.

Pros: Higher approval rates, more options.

Cons: Can be more complex to manage.

Key Players:

Momnt ChargeAfter GreenSky

Marketplace

An open platform where multiple lenders compete. Often free for the merchant, with a "no dealer fee" model.

Pros: "No dealer fees" is a huge selling point.

Cons: Cost passed to consumer (higher rates).

Key Players:

Acorn Finance Hearth

Key Competitive Pitches & How to Win

"We are the bank."

Pitch by: Synchrony, Wells Fargo

Their strength is stability and brand trust. They target established merchants who prioritize a relationship with a large bank.

Your Winning Counter

"Stability is crucial, but so is speed and maximizing sales. We offer bank-level security with the agility to approve more customers, instantly, without the lengthy onboarding process."

"We have ZERO dealer fees!"

Pitch by: Acorn Finance, Hearth

A very powerful and simple pitch. Their model is free for the contractor, making it highly attractive.

Your Winning Counter

"'Free for you' often means higher rates for your customer, which can hurt closing rates. We give you control over promotions, like 0% APR, to increase ticket sizes and make the sale."

"We approve everyone!"

Pitch by: Momnt, ClarityPay

They promise to find a 'yes' for every customer, which is a major pain point for merchants losing sales to declines.

Your Winning Counter

"Approving everyone is the goal. We do it through a smarter, more efficient platform that provides a better experience for you and your customer, with transparent costs and powerful tools to manage your pipeline."
